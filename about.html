About Roger and Jane

We believe AI is heralding a new world for small business and everyday work - one where practical automation and everyday AI tools are no longer just for large companies or technical specialists.

In March 2026 I went on holiday to Sri Lanka for two weeks with a driver-guide whose small tour business, Ceylon Adhi Tours, had been hit hard by the fallout from the Iran war - his bookings had dried up and he had no way to reach new customers. He asked me if I could help. I built him a new website in Next.js.

Doing that made me realise how little most small business owners know about what AI can actually do for them - not the hype, the practical stuff: better websites, automation, and tools that save time and win customers. Since then I've built websites for other small businesses facing the same problem.

The challenge I keep running into is credibility: why should a small business owner trust someone who is still learning this fast-changing field? The AI Apprentice is our answer to that - building in public, showing the work, and letting the results speak for themselves.

It's also an experiment in its own right. I wanted to see what happens when ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude collaborate on the same problem from the perspective of someone my age learning by doing - rather than any one of them just producing "the AI course" on its own.

Why Jane matters to the project

Jane represents an important test for the course: if the lessons are clear, useful, and practical for someone learning alongside the project, they are more likely to work for other people who feel curious but cautious about AI.

The project that started it

A Next.js website built for a Sri Lankan driver-guide whose tourism business lost its customers overnight - the first proof that a small, practical AI-built website can genuinely help someone.
