Starter resources

Keep these prompts and checks nearby. They are designed to help you get useful answers without pretending AI is always right.

Ten starter prompts

Copy, paste, and adapt these in ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or another AI assistant.

  • Explain simplyExplain [topic] as if I am hearing about it for the first time. Use plain language and one real-world example.
  • Check an answerHere is an answer I got from AI on [topic]. What parts might be wrong, outdated, or worth double-checking?
  • Sharpen a vague promptRewrite this request to be more specific. Add the audience, format, context, and goal: [your original request]
  • Improve its own answerReview your last answer. What is weak, vague, or missing? Rewrite a better version.
  • Draft an emailDraft a short, polite email to [recipient] about [topic]. Keep it under 150 words and end with a clear next step.
  • Summarise a documentSummarise the following in five bullet points a busy person could read in 30 seconds: [paste text]
  • Turn notes into action itemsTurn these notes into a numbered action list with an owner and due date for each item: [paste notes]
  • Improve customer copyRewrite this homepage message to be clearer and warmer for a small business audience: [paste text]
  • Compare two optionsCompare [option A] and [option B] in a pros-and-cons table. Include cost, risk, and what could go wrong with each.
  • Prepare for a decisionI am deciding between [options]. What questions should I ask before committing, and what assumptions am I making?

Three checks before trusting an AI answer

Check facts

Verify numbers, dates, names, legal claims, medical claims, prices, and anything that could have changed recently.

Check context

Ask what assumptions the AI made. It may be answering for the wrong country, audience, industry, or situation.

Check tone

Before sending customer-facing text, make sure it still sounds like you and does not overpromise.
