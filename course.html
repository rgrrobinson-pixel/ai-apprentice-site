The six-week course outline

One practical outcome each week, with three to five hands-on tasks. No jargon, no "master AI" hype - just useful skills you can use the same day.

1Understand AI and use it safely
▸

Outcome

You understand what AI can help with, where it can be wrong, and how to use it responsibly.

Tasks

  • Ask AI to explain a topic simply.
  • Check the answer against another source.
  • Identify where the answer may be uncertain.
  • Write a personal AI safety rule.

Example

You ask: "Explain what a business overdraft is, as if I'm hearing about it for the first time."
AI answers: a plain-language explanation, plus (if you ask) a worked example with made-up numbers.
Then you check it: "What in that answer could be wrong, outdated, or specific to one country's banking rules?" - AI will usually flag its own uncertainty once asked directly.

Try it yourself

This week's worksheet
  1. A topic I want AI to explain simply: ___________
  2. One thing in its answer I should double-check before relying on it: ___________
  3. My personal AI safety rule (e.g. "I always verify numbers, dates, and legal claims"): ___________
2Write better prompts
▸

Outcome

You can give AI clearer instructions and improve weak answers.

Tasks

  • Rewrite a vague prompt into a specific one.
  • Add context, audience, format, and goal.
  • Ask AI to improve its own answer.
  • Create five reusable prompts.

Example

Vague: "Write about marketing."
Specific: "Write a 100-word Facebook post for a small bakery announcing a new sourdough loaf. Audience: local customers. Tone: warm and friendly. Goal: get people to visit this weekend."
The second version gives AI an audience, a format, a tone, and a goal - so the answer comes back usable on the first try instead of generic.

Try it yourself

This week's worksheet
  1. A vague request I've made before: ___________
  2. Rewritten with audience + format + goal: ___________
  3. Ask AI: "What's weak about your last answer? Improve it." What changed: ___________
3Everyday workflows
▸

Outcome

You use AI for common work tasks, comfortably and quickly.

Tasks

  • Draft an email.
  • Summarise a long document.
  • Create a meeting agenda.
  • Turn notes into action items.
  • Rewrite text for a different audience.

Example

You paste: messy meeting notes - "talked about website, John will look at pricing, need new photos, maybe launch in March, Sarah checking supplier costs"
You ask: "Turn these into a numbered action list with an owner and a rough due date for each."
AI returns: a clean list - 1. Review website pricing (John, this week) - 2. Source new product photos (unassigned) - 3. Check supplier costs (Sarah, before March) - 4. Confirm March launch date (team, next meeting).

Try it yourself

This week's worksheet
  1. One real email, document, or set of notes I'll run through AI this week: ___________
  2. What I asked for: ___________
  3. What I changed by hand afterward (AI rarely gets it 100% right first time): ___________
4Marketing, websites, and customers
▸

Outcome

You understand how AI can help with customer-facing content.

Tasks

  • Improve a homepage message.
  • Draft social post ideas.
  • Create FAQ answers.
  • Write a customer reply.
  • Identify trust signals on a website.

Example

This is a real one: when I built a website for a Sri Lankan driver-guide whose tourism business had lost its customers (the full story is here), the original homepage just said "Sri Lanka tours and transport."
Rewritten with AI's help: "A private driver-guide for Sri Lanka - two weeks, one trusted guide, no tour buses."
Same business, same driver - but now it says who it's for and why it's different, which is what actually earns a click.

Try it yourself

This week's worksheet
  1. My (or a client's) current homepage message: ___________
  2. Ask AI: "Rewrite this to be clearer and warmer for [your actual customer]." Result: ___________
  3. One trust signal missing from the site (reviews, contact details, real photos): ___________
5Research and decision-making
▸

Outcome

You can use AI to compare options and support decisions without blindly trusting it.

Tasks

  • Compare two tools or suppliers.
  • Create a pros and cons table.
  • Ask for assumptions and risks.
  • Check sources.
  • Prepare questions before making a decision.

Example

You ask: "Compare Mailchimp and ConvertKit for a small tour business sending a monthly newsletter. Pros, cons, cost, and what could go wrong with each."
AI returns: a table covering price tiers, ease of use, and automation features - plus, if you ask directly, its own assumptions (e.g. "I'm assuming under 1,000 subscribers - costs change a lot above that").
The habit to build: always follow up with "what are you assuming, and what should I check myself?"

Try it yourself

This week's worksheet
  1. A real decision I'm facing right now: ___________
  2. Pros/cons table AI produced: ___________
  3. Assumption I need to verify myself before deciding: ___________
6Build a repeatable AI workflow
▸

Outcome

You leave with your own practical AI starter system.

Tasks

  • Choose one recurring task.
  • Build a step-by-step workflow.
  • Create reusable prompts.
  • Define quality checks.
  • Decide when human judgement is needed.

Example

Recurring task: replying to customer enquiries.
Workflow: 1) paste the enquiry into a saved prompt template -> 2) AI drafts a reply in your tone -> 3) you check pricing and dates are correct -> 4) you send it.
Quality check: never send a price, date, or availability claim without checking it yourself first. Human judgement needed: anything emotional, a complaint, or a first-time customer.

Try it yourself

This week's worksheet - your AI starter system
  1. Recurring task I'm choosing: ___________
  2. My reusable prompt template: ___________
  3. My quality check before anything goes out: ___________
  4. When I'll always do this myself instead of AI: ___________

What happens after Week 6?

The aim is not to learn every AI tool. The aim is to choose one useful workflow and keep improving it. By the end, you should know where AI genuinely helps, where it needs checking, and what you want to practise next.
